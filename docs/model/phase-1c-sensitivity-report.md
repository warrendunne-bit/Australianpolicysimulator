# Phase 1C sensitivity report

Baseline version: `australian-baseline-v1.0`  
Run date: 2026-07-12

## Sensitivity tested

Primary uncertain/high-materiality assumption tested: net overseas migration (NOM).

- Low case: 75% of central NOM.
- Central case: reviewed v1.0 NOM, 301,000 people/year.
- High case: 120% of the nearby FY2024-25 NOM comparison/high estimate.

## Interpretation

The sensitivity module runs the immigration current-path scenario with low, central and high NOM and reports 2036 outcomes for:

- housing stress index;
- labour supply index;
- federal budget pressure;
- social cohesion risk;
- most affected groups.

## Expected qualitative effect

- Higher NOM raises labour supply and population growth.
- Higher NOM tends to increase housing stress unless housing supply responds.
- Winners/losers change because employers, governments, renters, regions and services are affected through different pathways.
- Social cohesion conclusions are flagged as exploratory because causal evidence is weaker.

## Dominant uncertainty

NOM and housing completions dominate the current immigration scenario. Any policy conclusion about immigration is therefore conditional on housing supply and infrastructure/service capacity.

## Warning

Sensitivity output is not a prediction. It is a range test showing whether the scenario conclusion depends on low-confidence or volatile assumptions.
